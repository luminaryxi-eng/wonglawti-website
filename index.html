Understanding the Sale & Purchase Agreement (SPA) 了解房屋买卖合同（SPA）

Kevin Goh · Kuantan · 3 min read

The Sale & Purchase Agreement (SPA) is the most important legal document in any property transaction. Whether you are a seller or a buyer, understanding your rights and obligations before signing is essential. Below are the key things to consider from both sides of the transaction.

房屋买卖合同（SPA）是房产交易中最重要的法律文件。无论您是卖家还是买家，在签署合同之前了解自身的权利与义务至关重要。以下分别从卖家与买家的角度，列出签约前须知的关键事项。

卖

As a Seller — Things to Consider 卖家须知

1

If there is no remaining loan with the bank, the original title deed should be in your possession. Make sure it is in its original condition — no holes punched through it and not laminated.

若名下房产已无银行贷款，原始地契应由本人妥善保管。请确保地契完好无损——切勿有打孔或塑封的情况。

2

Determine whether you have owned the property for more than 5 years. If yes, the sale is not subject to Real Property Gains Tax (RPGT). If less than 5 years, the government will levy RPGT ranging from 15% to 30% depending on the holding period.

确认您持有该房产是否超过五年。若超过五年，出售时无需缴纳产业盈利税（RPGT）。若不足五年，则须依法缴税，税率介于 15% 至 30% 之间，视持有年限而定。

3

The seller must sign the SPA in person before a lawyer. This cannot be done by proxy.

卖家必须亲临律师楼，在律师见证下签署买卖合同，不得委托他人代签。

4

For a freehold property, the seller may collect the full 10% deposit upon signing of the SPA. For a leasehold property, the 10% deposit is held by the lawyer in a stakeholder account until the state authority's consent is approved.

若为永久地契（Freehold）房产，签署 SPA 时即可收取 10% 定金。若为租赁地契（Leasehold）房产，该 10% 定金须由律师代为保管，待州政府批准同意书后方可释出。

The seller cannot access the deposit for leasehold property until the consent is granted — this is standard practice to protect all parties.

在同意书获批前，卖家无法动用该笔款项——这是保障各方权益的标准做法。

5

The state authority consent process for leasehold properties typically takes 1 to 3 months, depending on the efficiency of the respective Land Office.

租赁地契房产的同意书申请，一般需要约 1 至 3 个月方可获批，视各地土地局的处理速度而定。

💡

Key takeaway: The handling of your deposit and the overall timeline differs significantly between freehold and leasehold properties. Always clarify this with your lawyer before signing.

重点提示：永久地契与租赁地契在定金处理及交割时间上存在显著差异，签约前务必与律师充分确认。

买

For the Buyer — Things to Consider 买家须知

1

For a second-hand property, inspect the property thoroughly. The seller has no obligation to repair or improve the property for you. The transaction is on an "as is where is" basis — what you see is what you get.

购买二手房时，务必亲自彻底检查房产状况。卖家并无义务修缮房屋，交易以「现况」（As Is Where Is）为基础——您所看到的，就是您买到的。

2

After agreeing to purchase, it is advisable to place a booking deposit with the agency or with your chosen lawyer. The booking deposit is normally 2–3% of the purchase price.

确认购买意向后，建议通过产业代理或您属意委托的律师楼缴付订金，以锁定房源。订金通常为购买价的 2% 至 3%。

3

After placing the booking, apply for a bank loan promptly. The agent must inform the seller that the buyer requires time to obtain loan approval, so that the seller is not kept waiting unreasonably.

缴付订金后，须立即着手向银行申请房屋贷款。产业代理须提前知会卖家，买家需要时间等待贷款批核，以避免不必要的纠纷。

4

Once the loan is approved, prepare the balance of the deposit (total deposit minus the booking already paid) and attend the lawyer's office to sign both the SPA and the loan documents.

贷款获批后，须准备余额定金（即总定金扣除已支付订金后的差额），并携同至律师楼签署 SPA 及贷款文件。

Example: If the total deposit is 10% and you have paid a 3% booking fee, you will need to pay an additional 7% when signing the SPA.

例如：若总定金为购价的 10%，而订金已付 3%，则签署 SPA 时须再支付 7% 余额。

5

For a leasehold property, the transaction cannot be completed until the state authority's consent is approved. The entire process will typically take more than 3 months.

若所购房产为租赁地契，交易须待土地局同意书获批方可继续推进，整个过程通常超过三个月。

6

For a freehold property, the entire process can generally be completed within 3 months from the date the SPA is signed.

若为永久地契房产，从签署 SPA 起，整个产权转让手续一般可在三个月内完成。

7

The transaction is deemed complete when the bank releases the loan funds to the lawyer's office. The lawyer will then notify the agent, and the buyer may proceed to collect the keys to the property.

当银行将贷款款项拨付至律师楼信托账户时，即表示交易正式完成。律师楼将通知代理，买家即可安排取得房屋钥匙。

8

It is not the lawyer's duty to change the names on electricity and water accounts. The buyer must do this themselves, as opening new utility accounts requires the payment of deposits.

水电账户更名并非律师的职责。买家须自行前往相关机构（如 TNB 及当地水务局）办理过户，因开设新账户需缴付押金。

Tip: Arrange for utility transfers as early as possible to ensure bills are issued under your name from the start.

建议尽早办妥水电更名，以确保账单名义从一开始便正确无误。

💡

Key takeaway: The property purchase process involves multiple stages with specific timelines. Prompt action at each step — especially on loan applications and document signing — helps ensure a smooth transaction.

重点提示：购房流程涉及多个阶段，每一步都有需要配合的时间节点。在贷款申请与文件签署等关键环节及时跟进，是顺利完成交易的关键。

Selling a Property. Pay Tax? 卖房需要缴税吗？

Kevin Goh · Kuantan · 3 min read

When you sell a property in Malaysia, you may be required to pay a tax known as the Real Property Gains Tax (RPGT). Understanding how RPGT works — and when it applies — can help you plan your sale more effectively and avoid unexpected costs.

01

What Is RPGT?

RPGT is a tax imposed by the Malaysian government on capital gains derived from the disposal (sale) of real property.

A capital gain arises when the resale price of a property exceeds the original purchase price — in other words, when you sell your property for more than what you originally paid for it.

If you sell a property at a loss or without any profit, RPGT is not payable.

02

How Is RPGT Calculated?

1

Step 1 — Calculate the Total Gain

Total Gain = Selling Price (the price you are selling to the current buyer) minus the Purchase Price (the price you originally paid when you bought the property).

To find your original purchase price, refer to your old Sale & Purchase Agreement (SPA). If you cannot locate it, you may apply to the Land Office to extract a copy of Form 14A.

2

Step 2 — Deduct Allowable Expenses

Net Gain = Total Gain minus Deductible Expenses

Deductible expenses may include:

  • Legal fees paid during the purchase or sale
  • Agent commission fees
  • Renovation costs
  • Other miscellaneous expenses related to the property

You must be able to provide documentary evidence for all deductible expenses — such as invoices and official receipts. Expenses without supporting documents cannot be deducted.

3

Step 3 — Apply the Individual Exemption

Every individual seller is automatically entitled to an exemption on their chargeable gain. The exemption amount is whichever is higher between:

  • RM 10,000, or
  • 10% of the Net Gain

Chargeable Gain = Net Gain − Exemption Amount

This exemption applies to individuals only, not companies. It is applied automatically — you do not need to apply for it separately.

4

Step 4 — Calculate RPGT Payable

RPGT Payable = Chargeable Gain × Applicable RPGT Rate

The rate depends on how long you have held the property before selling it (see the rate table below).

03

RPGT Rate Table

Holding Period Companies (%) Individuals (%)
Within 3 years of purchase 30% 30%
In the 4th year 20% 20%
In the 5th year 15% 15%
In the 6th year and beyond 10% 0% ✓

✓ Individual sellers who have held the property for more than 5 years are exempt from RPGT. This exemption does not apply to companies.

Keep all receipts and invoices for legal fees, agent fees and renovation works — every deductible expense reduces your taxable gain.

在马来西亚出售房产时，您可能须缴纳一种称为产业盈利税（Real Property Gains Tax，简称 RPGT）的税款。了解 RPGT 的运作方式及其适用情形，有助于您更有效地规划出售事宜，并避免意外支出。

01

什么是产业盈利税（RPGT）？

产业盈利税（RPGT）是马来西亚政府对房产出售所得资本收益征收的税款。

资本收益是指房产的转售价格高于原始购买价格时所产生的差额，即您以高于当初购入价的价格出售房产时所获得的利润。

若您出售房产时亏损或未获利润，则无需缴纳 RPGT。

02

产业盈利税如何计算？

1

第一步 — 计算总收益

总收益 = 售价（您出售给现任买家的价格）减去购买价（您当初购买时所支付的价格）。

如需查询原始购买价格，请参阅您当年的买卖合同（SPA）。若找不到相关文件，可前往土地局申请提取副本。

2

第二步 — 扣除可抵扣费用

净收益 = 总收益 减去 可抵扣费用

可抵扣费用包括：

  • 购买或出售时支付的律师费
  • 代理佣金
  • 装修费用
  • 其他与房产相关的杂项支出

所有可抵扣费用须提供书面证明文件，例如发票及官方收据。无凭证的费用不得申报扣除。

3

第三步 — 扣除个人豁免额

每位个人卖家均可自动享有应税收益豁免额。豁免金额为以下两者中较高者：

  • RM 10,000，或
  • 净收益的 10%

应税收益 = 净收益 − 豁免额

此豁免额仅适用于个人卖家，不适用于公司。豁免额将自动计算，无需另行申请。

4

第四步 — 计算应缴产业盈利税

应缴产业盈利税 = 应税收益 × 适用税率

税率取决于您持有房产至出售时的年数（详见下方税率表）。

03

Why Every Adult Should Have a Will为何每位成年人都应立遗嘱？

Kevin Goh · Kuantan · 3 min read

Most people put off writing a will because they assume it is complicated, expensive, or only necessary when you are old. None of that is true. A will is simply a written document that states what you want to happen to your belongings after you die — and without one, the law decides for you.

01

What Happens If You Die Without a Will?

Dying without a valid will is called dying intestate. When this happens, your estate is distributed strictly according to the Distribution Act 1958 — not according to your wishes.

The law sets fixed shares for your spouse, children and parents. There is no room for personal preference. A close friend, a sibling you wanted to provide for, or a charity you cared about — none of them will receive anything unless the law says so.

On top of that, your family will need to apply for Letters of Administration through the court, a process that takes longer and is more costly, before they can touch a single asset.

02

Five Good Reasons to Write a Will Today

1

You decide who gets what. A will lets you name specific beneficiaries for specific assets — your house to your spouse, your savings to your children, a keepsake to a sibling. Without a will, the law makes those choices.

2

You protect your minor children. If your children are under 18, a will lets you appoint a guardian of your choice. Without a will, the court decides who raises them.

3

You reduce family disputes. Clear written instructions significantly lower the risk of arguments over your estate. A will leaves little room for misunderstanding.

4

You speed up the process. With a valid will, your executor can apply for a Grant of Probate, which is generally faster and simpler than Letters of Administration. Your family gains access to your assets sooner.

5

You appoint someone you trust. A will lets you name an executor — the person responsible for carrying out your wishes. Choose someone reliable, organised and trustworthy.

03

Important: Who Can Make a Will?

Under the Wills Act 1959, a will may be made by any person who is of sound mind and has reached the age of majority (18 years old). A will made by a person under 18 is not valid.

Note: The Wills Act 1959 applies to West Malaysia only and does not apply to Muslims, whose testamentary matters are governed by Islamic law (Hukum Faraid).

04

What About EPF and Insurance Nominations?

Many people believe that EPF nominations and insurance policies are sufficient. They are not. EPF and insurance payouts go directly to nominees — but your other assets such as bank accounts, property and investments are not covered by nominations.

A will covers everything that nominations do not. Think of a will and your nominations as complementary — you need both.

💡

Bottom line: A will is one of the most important documents you will ever sign. It costs relatively little to prepare, takes effect only after your death, but gives you complete control over what happens to everything you have worked for. Do not leave it to chance — or to the law.

许多人迟迟不立遗嘱，原因不外乎觉得手续繁琐、费用昂贵，或认为只有年迈时才需要考虑。事实并非如此。遗嘱只是一份书面文件，写明您希望在身后如何处理名下财产——若没有遗嘱，法律将替您做决定。

01

没有遗嘱，会发生什么？

在没有有效遗嘱的情况下离世，法律上称为无遗嘱身故（Intestate）。此时，您的遗产将依据1958年《分配法令》严格分配，而非按照您本人的意愿。

法律对配偶、子女及父母各自设有固定份额，没有任何弹性空间。您想照顾的兄弟姐妹、挚友或您关心的慈善机构，若法律未有规定，将一无所获。

此外，您的家人须向法院申请遗产管理书（Letters of Administration），这一过程往往耗时数年，且需支付额外费用，方可动用名下任何资产。

02

立遗嘱的五大理由

1

您决定财产归谁。遗嘱让您为特定资产指定特定受益人——房产留给配偶、储蓄传给子女、纪念物赠予兄弟姐妹。没有遗嘱，这些决定将由法律代劳。

2

保护未成年子女。若您的子女未满18岁，遗嘱允许您亲自指定监护人。若无遗嘱，由谁抚养您的孩子将由法院裁决。

3

减少家庭纠纷。清晰的书面指示能大幅降低遗产争议的风险，让家人少一份矛盾，多一份安心。

4

加快遗产处理速度。持有有效遗嘱，遗嘱执行人可申请遗嘱认证令（Grant of Probate），程序一般比申请遗产管理书更快捷简便，家人可更早获得遗产。

5

指定您信任的人执行遗嘱。遗嘱让您委任一名遗嘱执行人（Executor），负责按照您的意愿处理身后事宜。请选择一位可靠、细心且值得信赖的人担任此职。

03

重要：谁可以立遗嘱？

根据1959年《遗嘱法令》，凡精神健全且已达成年年龄（18岁）的人士，均可立遗嘱。未满18岁者所立遗嘱，在法律上不具效力。

注意：1959年《遗嘱法令》仅适用于西马来西亚，且不适用于穆斯林。穆斯林的遗产事务受伊斯兰法律（法莱依法，Hukum Faraid）管辖。

04

公积金与保险受益人指定，够了吗？

许多人以为公积金（EPF）受益人指定及保险受益人已足够，实则不然。EPF 及保险理赔款项直接拨付给受益人，但您其他资产——如银行账户、房产及投资——并不在受益人指定的涵盖范围内。

遗嘱正好补充受益人指定所未涵盖的部分。两者相辅相成，缺一不可。

Requirements for a Valid Will Under Malaysian Law马来西亚法律下遗嘱的有效要求

Kevin Goh · Kuantan · 3 min read

A will is only legally effective if it satisfies the formal requirements set out in the Wills Act 1959 (Act 346). A document that does not comply — however sincerely intended — may be declared invalid, leaving your estate to be distributed as if no will existed. Here is what the law requires.

01

Who Can Make a Will

1

Age: The testator (the person making the will) must have reached the age of majority — 18 years old. A will made by a person under 18 is completely void.

Exception: Members of the armed forces, mariners or seamen on active service may make a privileged will regardless of age.

2

Sound Mind: The testator must be of sound mind at the time of making the will — meaning they understand the nature of making a will, the extent of their property, and who their natural beneficiaries are.

3

Not a Muslim: The Wills Act 1959 does not apply to Muslims. Muslim testamentary matters are governed by Islamic law (Hukum Faraid). This Act applies to West Malaysia only.

02

The Will Must Be in Writing

A will must be in writing. An oral statement of intention — however clearly expressed — is not a valid will under the Act.

Exception: A privileged will (for armed forces members and mariners on active service) may be oral.

03

Signed by the Testator

1

The will must be signed at the foot or end by the testator personally.

2

The signature must be made or acknowledged by the testator as their signature to the will. The testator must confirm that the signature on the document is theirs.

3

Any disposition or direction written after the signature is made has no legal effect — only what appears above the signature is valid.

04

Two Witnesses Required

1

The testator's signature must be made or acknowledged in the presence of at least two witnesses present at the same time. Both witnesses must then sign (subscribe) the will in the presence of the testator.

2

A witness must not be a beneficiary. If a witness (or their spouse) is named as a beneficiary in the will, that gift is void — though the will itself remains valid. The witness loses their gift but can still give evidence about the will's execution.

Practical tip: Always choose witnesses who are not named in your will and who are not the spouse of anyone named in your will.

3

A witness who is later found to be legally incompetent does not invalidate the will. The will remains valid even if a witness's competency is later questioned.

05

Alterations After Signing

Any alterations, additions or deletions made to a will after it has been signed and witnessed are not valid.

If you wish to change your will, the cleanest approach is to revoke the old will.

06

When Does a Will Become Revoked?

1

Marriage automatically revokes a will. A will is revoked upon the testator's marriage — unless the will was expressly made in contemplation of that specific marriage.

If you get married after making your will, make a new will immediately — your old will is no longer valid.

2

A will may also be revoked by: a later will or codicil, a written declaration of revocation executed with the same formality, or by the testator physically destroying the will (burning, tearing etc.) with the intention to revoke it.

3

Changed circumstances alone do not revoke a will. A will is not revoked merely because your circumstances change — for example, having more children, acquiring new property, or divorcing. You must take positive steps to change the will.

💡

Quick checklist — is your will valid? It must be: (1) in writing, (2) signed by the testator at the foot, (3) the signature acknowledged before two witnesses present simultaneously, (4) signed by both witnesses in the testator's presence, and (5) no beneficiary should be a witness. If any one of these is missing, the will may be invalid.

遗嘱只有在符合1959年《遗嘱法令》（第346法令）所订明的正式要求时，方具法律效力。一份不符合规定的文件——无论立遗嘱人意愿多么真诚——均可能被宣告无效，导致遗产依无遗嘱身故的规定分配。以下是法律的具体要求。

01

谁可以立遗嘱

1

年龄：立遗嘱人须达到成年年龄（18岁）。未满18岁者所立遗嘱，在法律上完全无效。

例外：现役军人、海员或水手可立特许遗嘱（Privileged Will），不受年龄限制。

2

精神健全：立遗嘱时，立遗嘱人须处于精神健全的状态，即能够理解立遗嘱的性质、名下财产的范围，以及其自然受益人为何人。

3

非穆斯林：1959年《遗嘱法令》不适用于穆斯林。穆斯林的遗产事务受伊斯兰法律（法莱依法）管辖。本法令仅适用于西马来西亚。

02

遗嘱须以书面形式订立

遗嘱须以书面形式订立。口头表达的意愿——无论表述多么明确——在法令下均不构成有效遗嘱。

例外：现役军人及海员所立的特许遗嘱（Privileged Will）可以口头形式订立。

03

须由立遗嘱人签署

1

遗嘱须由立遗嘱人本人在文件末尾签署。

2

立遗嘱人须确认签名为其本人所签。签名须在立遗嘱人认可下作出，以证明该签名确实代表其遗嘱意愿。

3

任何在签名之后添加的条款或指示，均不具法律效力——只有签名以上的内容方为有效。

04

须有两名见证人

1

立遗嘱人签名时，须有至少两名见证人同时在场。两名见证人须在立遗嘱人在场的情况下，在遗嘱上签署作证。

2

见证人不得为受益人。若见证人（或其配偶）在遗嘱中被列为受益人，该馈赠将被视为无效——但遗嘱本身仍然有效。见证人将失去其所获赠的财产，但仍可就遗嘱的签立提供证据。

实用建议：请选择未在遗嘱中列名的人士担任见证人，且其配偶亦不应在遗嘱中出现。

3

即使见证人事后被发现在法律上不具资格，亦不会导致遗嘱无效。遗嘱的效力不受见证人资格问题的影响。

05

签署后的更改

遗嘱签署及见证后，任何更改、增添或删除均不具效力。

若需更改遗嘱，最妥善的做法是撤销旧遗嘱。

06

遗嘱何时会被撤销？

1

结婚将自动撤销遗嘱。立遗嘱人结婚后，其遗嘱即告撤销——除非该遗嘱在订立时已明确表明是为预备该次婚姻而立。

若您在立遗嘱后结婚，请务必立即重新立嘱——旧遗嘱已不再有效。

2

遗嘱亦可通过以下方式撤销：以新遗嘱或附件遗嘱取代、以正式书面声明宣告撤销，或由立遗嘱人以焚烧、撕毁等方式销毁遗嘱，且须具有撤销的明确意图。

3

情况改变本身不会撤销遗嘱。遗嘱不会因立遗嘱人的生活情况改变而自动撤销——例如添丁、新增财产或离婚。立遗嘱人须主动采取措施更改遗嘱。

💡

快速检查清单——您的遗嘱是否有效？须符合以下条件：（1）以书面形式订立；（2）由立遗嘱人在末尾签署；（3）签名须在两名同时在场的见证人面前作出或确认；（4）两名见证人须在立遗嘱人面前签署作证；（5）受益人不得担任见证人。若有任何一项欠缺，遗嘱可能被视为无效。

免责声明：本文为1959年《遗嘱法令》部分条文的通俗概述，仅供一般资讯参考，不构成法律意见。请务必委托持牌律师草拟或审核您的遗嘱。

A will that does not meet the formal requirements of the Wills Act 1959 may be declared invalid. Always have your will drafted or reviewed by a licensed lawyer to ensure it is legally enforceable.

不符合1959年《遗嘱法令》正式要求的遗嘱可能被宣告无效。请务必委托持牌律师草拟或审核遗嘱，以确保其具有法律效力。

No Will? See Here没有遗嘱？请看这里

Kevin Goh · Kuantan · 3 min read

When a person dies without a valid will, they are said to die intestate. Their estate does not simply go to the people closest to them — it is divided strictly according to the Distribution Act 1958. The law sets out fixed shares depending on who survives the deceased.

01

Who Gets What? — Distribution Chart

Who survives the deceased? Spouse Issue (Children) Parent(s)
Spouse only All — —
Spouse + Parent(s), no children ½ — ½
Children only, no spouse, no parent — All —
Parent(s) only, no spouse, no children — — All
Spouse + Children, no parent ⅓ ⅔ —
Children + Parent(s), no spouse — ⅔ ⅓
Spouse + Children + Parent(s) ¼ ½ ¼
No spouse, no children, no parent Siblings → Grandparents → Uncles/Aunts → Government

"Issue" means children (and their descendants). Shares among children are equal. If a child has predeceased the parent but has living children of their own, those grandchildren step into the child's share.

02

Key Things to Know

1

The law decides — not you. Without a will, you have no say in who receives your estate. The fixed shares under the Act apply regardless of your personal wishes or relationships.

2

Issue always takes the substantial share. Once children are in the picture, they receive the largest portion. Even if a spouse and parent survive, the children's share is never less than half.

3

Government taking a share of your estate is a myth. Only in the rarest case where no surviving relative can be found in any of the listed categories will the estate go to the Government.

03

Can an Illegitimate Child Inherit? — What the Court Said

Federal Court Case Tan Kah Fatt & Anor v. Tan Ying [2023]

Short answer: Yes — an illegitimate child can inherit.

The Federal Court confirmed that the Distribution Act 1958 uses the word "issue" in Section 6 — not "children." This was a deliberate choice. The court found that "issue" has a broader, non-exhaustive meaning that includes illegitimate children.

The court also noted that this reading is consistent with Article 8 of the Federal Constitution, which guarantees equality — there is no rational reason to discriminate between children based on the circumstances of their birth.

What this means for you: If you have children outside of marriage and die without a will, those children may have a legal claim to your estate. Equally, if you are an illegitimate child, you may be entitled to inherit from a parent's estate even without a will. This makes having a clear, valid will even more important — so that you control exactly who receives what.

💡

The simplest solution: Write a will. The Distribution Act 1958 applies only when there is no valid will. A properly executed will lets you decide exactly how your estate is divided — the law steps in only when you leave no instructions behind.

非婚生子女可以继承遗产吗？——法院怎么说

联邦法院案例 Tan Kah Fatt & Anor v. Tan Ying [2023]

简短回答：可以——非婚生子女有权继承遗产。

联邦法院确认，1958年《分配法令》第6条使用的是「Issue」一词，而非「Children（子女）」。这是立法者的刻意选择。法院认为，「Issue」具有更广泛、非穷尽性的含义，涵盖非婚生子女。

法院亦指出，此解释符合《联邦宪法》第8条所保障的平等权利——在子女所受的待遇上，以出生情况作为区分依据，在逻辑上并无合理理据。

这对您意味着什么：若您在婚姻以外育有子女，而在没有遗嘱的情况下离世，该子女可能对您的遗产拥有法律上的继承权。同样，若您本身是非婚生子女，即使没有遗嘱，您也可能有权继承父母的遗产。这正是订立清晰、有效遗嘱的重要原因——让您自己决定财产的归属。

最简单的解决方法：立遗嘱。1958年《分配法令》仅在没有有效遗嘱时方才适用。一份合法订立的遗嘱，让您自主决定遗产如何分配——只有在您未留下任何指示时，法律才会介入。

My Boss Won't Pay My Salary. Can I Just Quit and Sue?老板不发薪水，我能辞职然后起诉吗？

Kevin Goh · Kuantan · 3 min read

The short answer is yes — but only if you do it correctly. Under Malaysian law, there is a legal concept called constructive dismissal that protects employees when an employer's conduct makes it impossible to continue working. Unpaid salary and withheld EPF contributions are exactly the kind of conduct that can trigger this right.

01

What Is Constructive Dismissal?

Constructive dismissal happens when an employer behaves so badly that the employee has no real choice but to resign. Even though the employee technically quits, the law treats it as if the employer dismissed them.

The key question is: did the employer commit a fundamental breach — meaning a serious violation that goes to the heart of the employment contract? If yes, the employee is entitled to walk out and file a claim.

02

Does Unpaid Salary Count? — A Real Case

Industrial Court Case · 2026 Victor Peh v. Trillion Cove Holdings Berhad & Ors

An employee was hired as a Senior Corporate Legal Adviser at RM20,000 per month. His employer then:

✗ Paid his June salary late — only on 26 July
✗ Did not pay his July and August salaries at all
✗ Deducted EPF contributions from his salary but never paid them to EPF — for 6 months straight
✗ The employer blamed "financial hardship" — but the Court found this was not a valid excuse

The employee followed up almost daily with HR, got no satisfactory response, and eventually resigned on 1 September 2023 by issuing a Notice of Constructive Dismissal.

The Court's decision: The Industrial Court found he was constructively dismissed without just cause. He was awarded RM408,000 in back wages (24 months' salary, less 15%).

03

What You Need to Prove

To successfully claim constructive dismissal, you must show all of the following:

1

The employer broke the employment contract. Non-payment of salary and failure to contribute to EPF are both clear breaches — unpaid salary is a violation of the Employment Act 1955, and non-remittance of EPF breaks both the employment contract and the EPF Act 1991.

2

The breach is serious enough. Not every workplace grievance qualifies. The breach must be a fundamental one — something that strikes at the core of the employment relationship. Not receiving your salary for over a month clearly meets this threshold.

3

You resigned because of the breach — not for other reasons. Your resignation letter (Notice of Constructive Dismissal) should clearly state that you are leaving due to the employer's conduct.

4

You acted promptly. Do not wait too long after the breach occurs. If you continue working for too long without complaint, the court may treat it as you having accepted the situation and waived your right to claim.

04

Practical Steps: What Should You Do?

1

Document everything. Keep all payslips, bank statements showing no salary received, email exchanges with HR, and your EPF statement showing missing contributions. Evidence is everything.

2

Give the employer a chance to fix it. Raise the issue in writing and give the employer reasonable time to remedy the situation. This also shows the court you acted in good faith.

3

Issue a Notice of Constructive Dismissal. When you resign, do so in writing and state clearly that you are resigning due to the employer's fundamental breach. This letter is crucial — it becomes the foundation of your claim.

4

File a representation within 60 days. After resigning, you have 60 days to file a representation for constructive dismissal with the Department of Industrial Relations (JTKSM). Missing this deadline means losing your right to claim.

The 60-day time limit is strict. Do not delay — consult a lawyer as soon as possible after resigning.

05

"The Company Was Broke" — Is That an Excuse?

No. The court in the Trillion Cove case was clear: financial hardship does not excuse an employer from paying salaries. Salary is not a gift — it is something the employee has already earned. An employer who cannot pay must address this through proper legal channels, not by simply keeping quiet and hoping employees stay.

💡

Key takeaway: If your employer stops paying your salary or withholds your EPF contributions, you do not have to just endure it. Malaysian law gives you the right to treat this as a dismissal, resign, and seek compensation. The critical things are to document the breach, act quickly, and file within 60 days of resigning.

简短回答是可以——但前提是您以正确的方式处理。在马来西亚法律下，有一个称为建设性解雇（Constructive Dismissal）的法律概念，当雇主的行为令雇员无法继续工作时，即可受到这一概念的保护。未付薪水与扣留公积金供款，正是触发这项权利的典型情形。

01

什么是建设性解雇？

建设性解雇是指雇主的行为恶劣至雇员别无选择，只能辞职的情形。尽管雇员在形式上是主动辞职，法律上却视为雇主将其解雇。

关键问题在于：雇主是否犯下了根本性违约——即严重违反劳动合同核心条款的行为？若是，雇员即有权离职并提出索赔。

02

未付薪水算数吗？——真实案例

劳资法庭案例 · 2026年 Victor Peh v. Trillion Cove Holdings Berhad & Ors

一名雇员以每月RM20,000的薪酬受聘为高级法律顾问。其雇主随后：

✗ 延迟支付6月薪水——迟至7月26日才发放
✗ 完全未支付7月及8月薪水
✗ 从薪水中扣除公积金供款，但连续6个月未将款项缴交公积金局
✗ 雇主以「财务困难」为由推搪——但法庭裁定此理由不能成立

该雇员几乎每天向人力资源部追问，却得不到任何令人满意的答复，最终于2023年9月1日发出建设性解雇通知书并即时辞职。

法庭裁决：劳资法庭裁定雇主无合理理由将其建设性解雇。雇员获判RM408,000的欠薪赔偿（24个月薪水，扣除15%）。

03

您需要证明什么？

要成功提出建设性解雇索赔，您须证明以下所有条件：

1

雇主违反劳动合同。未支付薪水及未缴纳公积金均属明确违约——未付薪水违反1955年《雇佣法令》，未缴公积金同时违反劳动合同及《雇员公积金法令1991》。

2

违约行为足够严重。并非每一项职场不满均符合条件。违约须属根本性——即触及雇佣关系核心的行为。超过一个月未收到薪水，显然达到这一门槛。

3

您是因为违约而辞职，而非其他原因。您的辞职信（建设性解雇通知书）须明确说明，您是由于雇主的违约行为而被迫辞职。

4

您须迅速采取行动。违约发生后不宜拖延。若您在没有投诉的情况下继续工作太长时间，法庭可能认定您已接受这一状况并放弃了索赔权利。

04

实际步骤：您应该怎么做？

1

记录所有证据。保存所有工资单、显示未收到薪水的银行对账单、与人力资源部的电邮往来记录，以及显示公积金缺供的公积金对账单。证据至关重要。

2

给予雇主纠正的机会。以书面形式提出问题，并给予雇主合理时间处理。这同时向法庭表明您是出于善意行事。

3

发出建设性解雇通知书。辞职时须以书面形式进行，并明确说明您是由于雇主的根本性违约而被迫辞职。这封信是您索赔的基础，切不可马虎。

4

于60天内提交申诉。辞职后，您有60天的时间向工业关系局（JTKSM）提交建设性解雇申诉。错过此期限，将丧失索赔权利。

60天的时限非常严格，不容拖延。辞职后请尽快咨询律师。

05

「公司财务困难」——可以作为借口吗？

不能。Trillion Cove案中，法庭立场明确：财务困难不能免除雇主支付薪水的责任。薪水并非恩赐——而是雇员已经用劳动换来的应得报酬。无力支付的雇主应通过合法途径处理，而非置若罔闻，期望雇员继续忍耐。

💡

重点总结：若雇主停止支付薪水或扣押您的公积金供款，您无需默默忍受。马来西亚法律赋予您将此视为解雇、辞职并寻求赔偿的权利。关键在于：记录违约行为、迅速采取行动，并于辞职后60天内提交申诉。
